Subject: one water mag corrosion AS Hamiltonian

Hey Nam,

Here are the coefficients for the AS Hamiltonian of one water on the slab in the reactant configuration.  10 occupied orbitals and 40 virtual.  For both geometries I've sent, I'm not sure if 15 and 10 is the optimal number of occupied orbitals to include (convergence in the energy difference isn't monotonic in the number of occupieds - less can be better in some cases).  In the future, we can run an MP2 calculation (cheaper than CCSD, but better than DFT) for the whole slab system and use that as a baseline to look for convergence in our active space MP2 energy differences between reactant and product.  This could help us select optimal active spaces, and should work for these systems with less than 70 atoms (not totally sure yet, but I expect it to work).
